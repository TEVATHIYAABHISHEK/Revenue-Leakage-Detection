# Revenue Leakage Detection Analysis

## Overview

Revenue leakage is one of those business problems that often goes unnoticed until it starts impacting profitability. In this project, I analyzed sales transaction data to identify where the business was losing money through excessive discounts, pricing inefficiencies, and loss-making transactions.

Using Excel, I built a revenue leakage analysis framework that quantifies losses, identifies root causes, and provides recommendations that could help improve profit margins and support better decision-making.

---

## Business Objective

The objective of this project was to answer the following questions:

* How much revenue is being lost?
* Which transactions are causing the losses?
* Are discounts contributing to revenue leakage?
* Which product categories are most affected?
* What actions can be taken to reduce future losses?

## Analysis Approach

The project was completed in the following stages:

1. Cleaned and prepared the raw sales dataset.
2. Calculated profitability and profit margins for each transaction.
3. Identified loss-making transactions using business rules.
4. Measured total revenue leakage and leakage percentage.
5. Classified leakage into key root-cause categories.
6. Analyzed category-level and transaction-level performance.
7. Generated business recommendations based on findings.

## Key Findings

* Identified transactions that negatively impacted profitability.
* Quantified the overall revenue leakage present in the dataset.
* Found patterns linking excessive discounts with loss-making sales.
* Highlighted categories that contributed the most to leakage.
* Provided actionable recommendations to reduce future revenue loss.

---

## Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis:

* Review high-discount transactions before approval.
* Establish minimum profit margin thresholds.
* Monitor recurring loss-making products.
* Improve pricing controls for high-risk categories.
* Implement regular revenue leakage monitoring processes.
